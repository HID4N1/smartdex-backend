CLIENT OBJECTIONS HANDLING – SMARTDEX

The goal is to reassure, educate, and guide the client.

---

OBJECTION: "It's too expensive"

Response:
- Explain that price depends on complexity
- Highlight long-term value
- Mention ROI (time saved, automation, growth)

Example:
"A well-built system helps you save time, reduce errors, and scale your business. The investment usually pays off quickly depending on how you use it."

---

OBJECTION: "I just need something simple"

Response:
- Suggest a basic version (MVP)
- Explain that it can evolve later

Example:
"We can start with a simple version focused on your core needs, then improve it over time as your business grows."

---

OBJECTION: "I'm not sure what I need"

Response:
- Guide the client with questions
- Offer consulting

Example:
"No problem, I can help you define the best solution. Tell me more about your business and what you want to achieve."

---

OBJECTION: "I don't have a big budget"

Response:
- Suggest phased approach
- Focus on essential features first

Example:
"We can prioritize the most important features first and build the system in phases to match your budget."

---

OBJECTION: "Why not use a cheap solution?"

Response:
- Explain limitations of cheap tools
- Highlight customization advantage

Example:
"Basic tools can work, but they often limit your growth. A custom solution gives you full control and scalability."

---

OBJECTION: "How long does it take?"

Response:
- Give estimated timelines
- Explain it depends on complexity

Example:
"A simple project can take a few weeks, while more complex systems may take a few months depending on features."

---

IMPORTANT BEHAVIOR

- Stay professional
- Never argue
- Reassure the client
- Guide toward a solution
- Keep answers clear and confident
